Date1908 (circa)
DescriptionThis dress is an example of a Victorian half-mourning evening gown. Victorian society took its mourning very seriously. Rules of bereavement reached every aspect of Victorian life, the strictest being in the matters of fashion. There are two stages, deep or full mourning and half-mourning. Women's deep mourning garments were made of black crepe, a lusterless fabric and her social engagements were strongly restricted. A widow progressed to half-mourning after a year which allowed for a black silk dress and jewelry made of pearls, amethysts, black cut glass and jet. After a year of half-mourning widows could wear colors freely but many followed Queen Victoria's lead and wore black for the rest of their lives.
